Term Life — Governance Rules

Term Life sold through a bank's corporate agent channel is the highest-risk product for mis-selling via an AI agent because the agent can quote, propose, and issue without a human salesperson in the loop. These rules govern what the Term Life agent is permitted to do and under what conditions it must halt.

Why this matters for the business: The Finance Minister, IRDAI Chairman, and RBI Governor have all individually flagged bancassurance mis-selling in 2024–25. An AI agent that issues Term Life without documented suitability evidence is the single highest-risk action point for an IRDAI enforcement action or RBI advisory notice.
TL-01
Suitability Gate — Needs-Based Assessment Mandatory
Agent cannot proceed to proposal without completing and logging a needs assessment
TERM HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
The agent must collect, validate, and record a suitability assessment — covering income, financial dependents, existing coverage, and stated protection need — before generating any policy proposal or premium quote for a customer. Absence of any one field = hard block on proposal generation.
Why Regulators Mandate This
IRDAI's Protection of Policyholders' Interests Regulations 2024 §5.2 mandates a board-approved suitability policy for all life insurance and annuity products. The September 2024 Master Circular operationalises this — every insurer and its distribution channel (including AI agents acting as corporate agent arms) must assess suitability before sale. Banks already receive more mis-selling complaints than any other channel per IRDAI's own annual report. An AI agent that skips suitability and issues directly is legally equivalent to a human agent doing the same — the bank/insurer bears joint liability.

TL-02
Benefit Illustration Delivery Before Proposal Acceptance
Customer must receive and acknowledge a CIS + benefit illustration before the agent advances to purchase
TERM HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
After a proposal is generated, the agent must deliver a Customer Information Sheet (CIS) and customised benefit illustration to the customer and obtain an explicit digital acknowledgment before calling buyProductClient or any purchase/issuance tool. The acknowledgment must be timestamped and stored with policy provenance.
Why Regulators Mandate This
The IRDAI Master Circular on Life Insurance Products (June 2024) mandates benefit illustrations for every life insurance product at point of sale. The Master Circular on Policyholder Protection (Sep 2024) mandates a CIS for all products — signed/acknowledged by both the customer and the authorized representative. In an AI-agent flow, the "authorized representative" is the agent itself — meaning the agent must generate the CIS, deliver it (digitally), and capture acknowledgment before any purchase action. Failure = the policy is issued without mandated pre-sale disclosure, creating grounds for claim rejection disputes and regulator action.

TL-03
Sum Assured Ceiling — Income Multiple Hard Cap
Agent cannot issue or propose coverage that exceeds regulatory underwriting limits without referral
TERM HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
The agent must compute Human Life Value (HLV) from declared income and cap the proposed sum assured at the insurer's pre-filed underwriting ceiling (typically 25–30x annual income for standard term without medical underwriting). Any proposal exceeding this ceiling must be blocked and routed to underwriting — the agent cannot proceed to issuance regardless of customer willingness to pay.
Business & Regulatory Logic
Over-insurance is a specific risk flagged in IRDAI underwriting guidelines. In a bancassurance context, an AI agent operating with no human underwriter in the loop is most likely to over-issue — especially when the loan amount creates an implicit pressure for high coverage. An agent that issues ₹2 Cr sum assured on ₹4L annual income with no medical check is a regulatory and claims liability. This rule forces the agent to check HLV math before every issuance.

Enforcement Action
HARD BLOCK + REFERRAL
Agent stops issuance, creates a referral case with the underwriting team, and informs the customer that their coverage request requires additional review. No partial issuance is permitted.

TL-04
Medical Underwriting Referral Trigger
Declared health conditions must route to medical underwriting — agent cannot override or ignore disclosures
TERM CONDITIONAL
▾
The Rule
When the proposal form contains any declaration of pre-existing medical conditions (diabetes, hypertension, cancer history, cardiac conditions, or any "Yes" on medical disclosure fields), the agent must pause issuance and route the proposal to a licensed medical underwriter. The agent cannot substitute its own judgment, modify the declaration, or proceed on the assumption that the condition is "minor."
Why This Is Non-Negotiable
Concealment or ignoring of pre-existing conditions is the single largest source of life insurance claim repudiation. An AI agent that sees a PED declaration and issues anyway creates: (a) a policy that will likely be contested at claim, (b) a regulatory violation (IRDAI mandates proper underwriting for PED declarations), and (c) criminal exposure for the corporate agent for knowingly issuing a policy with incomplete underwriting. The customer facing a claim rejection is exactly the outcome regulators are trying to prevent.

TL-05
Replacement & Churning Detection
Agent must flag when a customer is being proposed a new policy that replaces an existing one held with the same or another insurer
TERM HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
If the customer's suitability data or declaration indicates they hold an existing term life policy (same or competing insurer), and the proposed product provides materially similar or lesser coverage at the same or higher premium, the agent must flag this as a potential replacement case. The agent must document a specific benefit justification for the new policy and obtain explicit customer acknowledgment of the replacement before issuance. It cannot proceed based on a generic acknowledgment.
Why Regulators Flag This
Policy churning in bancassurance is one of IRDAI's stated top concerns. Banks have an incentive to sell new policies because they earn commission on first-year premiums. An AI agent, if unconstrained, will optimise for closure — it has no natural resistance to replacing a perfectly good policy if the customer is willing. IRDAI's regulations and the RBI mis-selling framework both identify replacement selling as a mis-selling risk requiring specific disclosure and documentation.
Enforcement Action
HARD BLOCK ON SILENT REPLACEMENT
If existing coverage is declared and proposed SA is within 20% of existing SA — the agent cannot proceed without: (a) generating a side-by-side comparison, (b) documenting the benefit justification for replacement, (c) recording explicit customer acknowledgment of the comparison. No issuance without all three.
Regulatory References
IRDAI Circular on Mis-Selling IRDAI Corporate Agent Regs 2015 RBI Draft Directions on Sales Practices 2026

Credit Life — Governance Rules

Credit Life is the most heavily regulated product in the bancassurance stack right now. RBI's February 2026 Draft Directions on Sales Practices specifically target loan-linked insurance bundling, force-selling, and insurer choice restriction. An AI agent operating at loan disbursement is in the most exposure-heavy position possible.

The regulatory moment in real time: RBI's proposed mis-selling norms are expected to have a 10–20% impact on banks' credit life business. Regulatory frameworks under IRDAI and RBI make it clear that borrowers cannot be compelled to opt for a specific insurer, though ground-level practices have often reflected otherwise. An AI agent operating without these rules is the highest-risk point of regulatory exposure in your entire product stack.
CL-01
Decoupling Gate — Credit Life Cannot Be Presented as Loan Condition
Agent language, flow, and data must treat insurance as optional — never as a prerequisite for loan approval or disbursement
CREDIT HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
The agent is prohibited from using any language, conditional logic, or data flow that presents credit life insurance as required for loan disbursement, lower interest rate, faster processing, or any other loan benefit. Insurance must be presented as a standalone protection option after the loan is confirmed. The disbursement trigger in the loan system must be independent of the insurance decision.
Why This Is the #1 Regulatory Priority for Credit Life
This is where the regulator and the press are currently focused. RBI guidelines clarify that the purchase of insurance products, while beneficial, is not mandatory for loan approval, ensuring that borrowers are not unduly pressured into buying insurance from their lenders. The practice of force-selling unrelated insurance under the garb of lending has raised concerns of fairness, transparency, and accountability in the financial sector. An AI agent that routes a borrower through an insurance flow before confirming loan disbursement is systematically replicating the exact practice the regulator is trying to eliminate.

"Complete your loan" CTA gated behind insurance stepExplicit bundling — RBI Draft Directions §Compulsory Bundling

Enforcement Action
HARD BLOCK — AGENT CANNOT EXECUTE BUNDLED FLOW
If agent detects that its insurance flow is being triggered as part of a loan disbursement sequence (same session, same customer, loan status = pending), it must: (a) complete or defer the insurance interaction to a separate, independent session; (b) confirm loan disbursement is not contingent on outcome; (c) log the decoupling confirmation in provenance trail.

RBI Draft Directions on Sales Practices Feb 2026 RBI Home Loan Guidelines 2024 (Insurance Optional) IRDAI Corporate Agent Regs 2015 IRDAI PPI Regs 2024
CL-02
Process Separation — Loan Journey vs Insurance Journey Must Be Distinct
System-level separation, not just language — two distinct session contexts required
CREDIT HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
The credit life agent must operate in a session context that is architecturally separate from the loan origination system. No shared state, no shared action triggers, no shared completion events between the loan workflow and the insurance workflow. Proof of separation must be available in the agent's provenance log on request by the regulator.
Regulatory Driver
Credit life is wrapped with the mortgage business and both cannot be clubbed in a single process. Banks will have to separate the process. This is not a soft recommendation — it is the expected outcome of the RBI's proposed directions. RBI's Draft Directions require explicit consent, prohibition on compulsory bundling, and compensation liability for unsuitable financial product sales. Process separation is the architectural enforcement of that prohibition.

CL-03
Premium Financing Cap — Single Premium Into Loan Amount
When credit life premium is rolled into the loan, the agent must enforce LTV and proportionality limits
CREDIT HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
If the customer elects to finance the single premium by rolling it into the loan, the agent must: (a) check that the resulting loan amount does not breach the insurer's or lender's LTV ceiling; (b) disclose the total additional interest cost of financing the premium over the loan tenure; (c) offer the alternative of paying premium out-of-pocket. The agent cannot default to premium financing without explicit customer selection.
Business & Regulatory Logic
Single-premium credit life rolled into the loan is a common practice — and a common source of complaints. The customer often does not realise they are paying interest on the insurance premium for the full loan tenure. In home loan scenarios, the RBI has set specific LTV ratios that cannot be breached. Including the single premium in the loan without disclosing its cost impact and without checking LTV compliance is both a mis-selling risk and an LTV guideline violation.

Enforcement Action

CL-04
Insurer Choice Optionality — No Tied or Default Selling
Agent must present insurer options; it cannot default to the bank's tied insurer or suppress alternatives
CREDIT HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
Under the open architecture bancassurance model, the agent must present the customer with the available credit life products from different insurers tied to the bank's corporate agent license. It cannot surface only one insurer's product by default, cannot rank or weight options based on commission rates, and must present comparison on coverage, premium, and exclusions before the customer selects.
Regulatory Logic
The open architecture model allows banks to offer products from different insurers, improving customer choice. It also requires strong governance to manage complexity and avoid bias in product recommendations. An AI agent, by its nature, will surface products in a ranked order. That ranking is a recommendation — and under IRDAI's suitability framework, recommendations must be based on the customer's needs, not on the insurer's commission rate. The agent's product ranking algorithm must be auditable and must not embed commission weighting.
Governance Requirements
RequirementImplementation

Default selectionNo product can be pre-selected; customer must actively choose

Enforcement Action
HARD BLOCK ON SINGLE-OPTION FLOW
If only one insurer product is surfaced and it is the bank's primary tied insurer, and other products are available on the platform — this is a policy violation. Agent must surface all available products. Provenance log must record: "Products presented: [list]. Ranking basis: [criteria]. Commission rates: [not used in ranking]."

CL-05
Coverage Proportionality — Policy Amount vs Outstanding Principal
Agent must flag over-coverage: credit life SA materially exceeding outstanding loan balance is a red flag
CREDIT CONDITIONAL
▾
The Rule
The sum assured proposed for credit life must be proportional to the outstanding loan principal. If the proposed sum assured exceeds the loan principal by more than 10%, the agent must flag this discrepancy, explain the mismatch to the customer, and obtain explicit acknowledgment before issuance. Over-coverage in credit life serves no purpose for the customer and may indicate that the agent is being used to inflate premium revenue.
Product Design Logic
Credit life's defining characteristic is that the death benefit is directly tied to the loan balance. Credit life insurance is a type of life insurance that kicks in to pay off any outstanding debt in the event that the borrower dies before the loan is paid in full. The policy's death benefit is directly tied to the loan balance. If the sum assured materially exceeds the loan — the excess benefit goes to the nominee, not the lender. While not necessarily harmful to the customer, it changes the product's nature and may indicate an upselling pattern that regulators would flag as inconsistent with the product's stated purpose.
Enforcement Action
CONDITIONAL — DISCLOSURE AND ACKNOWLEDGMENT REQUIRED
Agent surfaces the discrepancy: "The coverage you've selected (₹X) exceeds your loan balance (₹Y) by [Z%]. The excess ₹[amount] will be paid to your nominee, not toward the loan. Do you want to proceed?" — Explicit "Yes, I understand and want to proceed" required before issuance. Logged with timestamp.
Regulatory References
IRDAI PPI Regs 2024 (Full Disclosure) IRDAI Underwriting Guidelines

Shared Rules — Both Products, Bancassurance Channel

These rules apply to both the Term Life and Credit Life agents. They are grounded in the RBI FREE-AI Framework (Aug 2025), IRDAI Master Circular on Policyholder Protection (Sep 2024), RBI Draft Directions on Sales Practices (Feb 2026), and the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023.

BC-01
Customer Information Sheet (CIS) Delivery Gate
Mandatory pre-sale document — agent cannot issue without delivery confirmation
BOTH HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
A Customer Information Sheet (CIS) — a plain-language summary of inclusions, exclusions, premium, and key terms — must be delivered to the customer and acknowledged before any purchase tool is invoked. Acknowledgment must be affirmative (not a timed-out default). The CIS must be product-specific, not generic.
Regulatory Basis
IRDAI requires insurers to provide a Customer Information Sheet for all insurance products. A CIS is a document provided by the insurer along with the policy document that explains in simple words the basic features of a policy including inclusions, exclusions, and key terms. Acknowledgment in physical or digital form must be obtained from the customer. On request, CIS will be made available in local language.
Product-Specific CIS Requirements
Term Life CIS Must Include
  • Sum assured and coverage period
  • Premium amount and frequency
  • Exclusions (suicide clause, war, aviation)
  • Free-look period (30 days)
  • Claim process and contact
Credit Life CIS Must Include
  • Coverage = loan outstanding at time of death
  • Whether premium is single or regular
  • Whether premium is financed into loan
  • What happens to excess benefit (if any)
  • Exclusions (pre-existing conditions, suicide)
Enforcement Action
HARD BLOCK
buyProductClient call is rejected at the governance layer if CIS delivery + acknowledgment flag is not set to TRUE in the session state. No exceptions.

BC-02
Free-Look Period Disclosure — 30 Days Universal
Agent must disclose and confirm understanding of the 30-day free-look period before issuance
BOTH HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
Before issuing any policy, the agent must explicitly inform the customer of their 30-day free-look right — in plain language — and must record their acknowledgment. The agent cannot treat this as a footnote or small print. The disclosure must be a distinct, prominent step in the agent's flow.
Regulatory Basis
Policyholders now have a 30-day free-look period for life and health insurance policies. This allows them to review and understand the policy terms and conditions, with the option to cancel if they are unsatisfied. This applies regardless of how or where the policy is sold — including AI-agent-mediated digital sales. The free-look right exists to protect customers who may have been pressured or confused during the sales process, which is a specific risk in an AI-agent bancassurance context.

After issuance confirmation"Your policy is issued. You have until [date = today + 30] to cancel it for a full refund. To cancel, [contact method]."
Enforcement Action
HARD BLOCK
Issuance blocked if free-look disclosure step is not logged as completed.

BC-03
Nominee Completeness Gate — Policy Cannot Issue Without Full Nominee Details
Bank account details AND nominee name/relationship/DOB required before issuance
BOTH HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
A policy cannot be issued with incomplete nominee information. Required fields: nominee name, relationship to policyholder, date of birth, and contact number. For minor nominees, a trustee/appointee must also be recorded. An incomplete nominee record at issuance is the primary cause of delayed or disputed claim settlements — and the regulator explicitly mandates nominee completeness at point of sale.
Regulatory Basis
IRDAI's 2024 regulations require not just the nominee's name but bank account details too. Incomplete nominee information leads to delayed claims, which creates angry clients and lost referrals. In the bancassurance context, this is especially critical for credit life — where the death benefit should go to the lender (to settle the loan) and any residual to the nominee. If nominee details are incomplete, claim settlement is delayed and disputed.
Enforcement Action
HARD BLOCK
Agent cannot call buyProductClient or any issuance tool if nominee name, relationship, or DOB is null or empty. For minor nominees, trustee field must also be non-null. Agent returns: "To issue your policy, we need your nominee's complete details."
Regulatory References
IRDAI PPI Regs 2024 (Nominee Requirements) Insurance Act 1938 §39
BC-04
AI Disclosure to Customer — Mandatory at Session Start
Customer must be informed they are interacting with an AI agent before any sales interaction begins
BOTH HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
At the start of every insurance interaction, the agent must identify itself as an AI system — not as a human advisor, not as "our team," not as a named persona that implies human presence. The customer must be offered the option to speak with a human advisor before the sales process begins. This disclosure must be logged.
Regulatory Basis
The RBI FREE-AI framework places consumer protection on equal footing with innovation. Under the Protection pillar, institutions are required to embed safeguards including bias prevention and detection of harmful outputs. More directly, the most important obligations for regulated entities include ensuring all AI data practices strictly comply with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and developing robust mechanisms for detecting, escalating, and reporting AI-related incidents. An AI agent that impersonates a human advisor is a deceptive practice under both DPDP and IRDAI's mis-selling framework — informed consent cannot exist if the customer does not know they are interacting with an AI.
Required Disclosure Language
"Hi, I'm an AI assistant helping you explore insurance options from [Bank Name] in partnership with [Insurer Name]. I'm not a human advisor. If you'd prefer to speak with a person, you can reach our team at [contact]. Otherwise, I'm happy to help you find the right coverage."
Enforcement Action
HARD BLOCK
Agent session cannot advance to product recommendation or suitability questions until AI disclosure is delivered and the session timestamp is recorded. If the customer requests a human, the agent must provide contact information and cannot attempt to retain the customer with further sales engagement.
Regulatory References
RBI FREE-AI 2025 — Pillar: Protection DPDP Act 2023 (Informed Consent) IRDAI PPI Regs 2024 (Mis-Selling Prohibition)
BC-05
Affirmative Consent — No Pre-Ticked Options, No Passive Consent
Every material consent must be an unambiguous affirmative action by the customer
BOTH HARD BLOCK
▾
The Rule
At every consent checkpoint — purchase decision, data sharing, premium financing, nominee confirmation — the customer must take an active, unambiguous affirmative action (explicit "Yes, I confirm" or equivalent). No pre-ticked checkboxes, no "silence = consent," no "if you don't respond within X seconds we'll proceed," no "by continuing you agree." Each consent must be individually recorded with timestamp.
Regulatory Basis
RBI's Draft Directions require explicit consent, wherever required, to be based on unambiguous affirmative action. This is directly borrowed from DPDP Act 2023 consent standards and is now being applied to all sales practices by regulated entities. In an AI-agent flow, the most common consent failure modes are: timed-out defaults, bundled consent (one tick for many things), and implied consent through continued interaction. All three are prohibited.

BC-06
Cryptographic Provenance — Every Material Decision Logged and Immutable
The agent must maintain a tamper-evident audit trail of every action, decision, and consent in the session
BOTH MONITOR
▾
The Rule
Every material action the agent takes — every suitability question asked and answered, every product recommended and on what basis, every consent recorded, every tool call invoked — must be logged in a timestamped, append-only provenance record. This record must be producible to the regulator within 24 hours of a request. The record must state what the agent decided, why, and on whose authority.
Regulatory Basis
RBI FREE-AI's most important obligations include establishing a board-approved AI policy and implementing structured governance throughout the AI lifecycle, covering everything from model approval and testing to deployment and change control, with independent validation and periodic audits. The framework requires a named executive accountable for AI risk, with quarterly board reporting, and an audit and impact-assessment programme as a repeatable framework for periodic independent review. BC-07
Human Escalation Gate — Edge Profiles Require Human Review
AI agent must not independently handle customers who fall outside its designed operating envelope
BOTH CONDITIONAL
▾
The Rule
When the agent encounters a customer profile or request that falls outside its configured operating parameters, it must escalate to a human and cannot proceed unilaterally. The agent must have a defined escalation path, a defined escalation threshold, and must never communicate uncertainty to the customer in a way that causes distress or confusion.
Regulatory Basis
The RBI FREE-AI framework suggests a tolerant approach for low-risk AI use cases, particularly those that advance financial inclusion, but higher-stakes deployments will be subject to tighter scrutiny. Human-in-the-loop is a core governance requirement for high-stakes financial decisions. An AI agent making an underwriting decision on an edge case — and getting it wrong — is not just a bad outcome for the customer, it is a regulatory failure for the bank and insurer.
Edge Profile Triggers for Escalation
Term Life Escalation Triggers
  • Age > 60 or < 18
  • Any PED declared (TL-04)
  • Proposed SA > HLV ceiling (TL-03)
  • Replacement case (TL-05)
  • Customer expressing confusion or distress
Credit Life Escalation Triggers
  • Loan amount > ₹1 Cr (insurer-specific threshold)
  • Customer disputes insurer recommendation
  • Multi-borrower loan (joint life required)
  • NRI or foreign national borrower
  • Customer asks about grievance process
Enforcement Action
CONDITIONAL — ESCALATE TO HUMAN
Agent pauses the session, provides the customer with an estimated callback or connection time, and creates a handoff record with full session context for the human agent. Agent does NOT make a pending decision or communicate a tentative outcome.

RBI FREE-AI 2025 — Pillar: Governance RBI FREE-AI 2025 — Pillar: Protection
BC-08
Commission Conflict Disclosure — Recommendation Basis Must Be Auditable
Agent's product ranking must not embed commission weighting; if it does, this must be disclosed
BOTH CONDITIONAL
▾
The Rule
The criteria by which the agent ranks or recommends products must be documented, versioned, and available for audit. If commission rates from insurers are a factor in product ranking, this must be explicitly disclosed to the customer. The default position must be that ranking is based solely on suitability match, premium-to-coverage ratio, and exclusion alignment — not commission. Any deviation from this requires board-level approval and disclosure.
Regulatory Basis
The 2024 EOM Regulations put more emphasis on governance, disclosure, and policyholder protection, including Board oversight and periodic reviews of commission structures. The regulations set expectations on transparency and prudence around all operating spends, including commissions. IRDAI proposed a transaction-fee model in May 2025 where fees would be directly charged to the borrower, with the objective of reducing conflicts of interest and making insurance sales more aligned with customer needs rather than the earning potential of the bank. An AI agent that embeds commission bias in its recommendation engine without disclosure is precisely the conflict of interest this regulatory trend is targeting.
Enforcement Action
CONDITIONAL — AUDIT TRIGGER
Quarterly audit of agent recommendation logs vs commission rate data. If correlation between recommendation frequency and commission rate exceeds 0.7, compliance alert triggered and ranking algorithm must be reviewed. Any undisclosed commission-based ranking = policy violation requiring remediation and regulator disclosure.
